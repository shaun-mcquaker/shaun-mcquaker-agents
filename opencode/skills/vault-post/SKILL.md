## Workflow

### 1. Gather Context

Before drafting, collect:

- **What happened?** The event, launch, milestone, or insight.
- **Who was involved?** People to credit or tag.
- **Why does it matter?** Impact on the team, org, or Shopify.
- **What are the numbers?** Metrics, dates, before/after comparisons.
- **Any artifacts?** Links to PRs, dashboards, docs, or screenshots.

Use Vault tools (`vault_get_project`, `vault_get_user`, `vault_get_team`, etc.) to pull real data. **Never fabricate metrics, dates, or project details.**

### 2. Determine Metadata

Every Vault post needs:

- **Post type** — see Post Types below
- **Audience** — who needs to see this (team, discipline, org, company)
- **Tone** — celebratory, informative, reflective, or urgent
- **Length** — short (2-3 paragraphs), medium (4-6), or long (narrative)

### 3. Draft

Write the post following the structure for the chosen post type. Apply the engagement patterns and formatting guidelines below.

### 4. Review

Before presenting the draft:

- [ ] All names spelled correctly and Vault profiles linked
- [ ] All metrics verified against real data (no fabrication)
- [ ] Hook is strong — would you stop scrolling?
- [ ] Post has a clear "so what" — the reader knows why this matters
- [ ] No corporate filler or empty superlatives
- [ ] Length is appropriate for the content
- [ ] Call to action is clear (if applicable)

### 5. Post

Present the draft to Shaun for review. **Never publish directly.** Iterate based on feedback.

## Post Types

### Hype / Celebration

For launches, milestones, wins, and recognition.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** — Lead with the result or the moment.
2. **Context** — What was the challenge or goal?
3. **Credit** — Name the people who made it happen.
4. **Impact** — Numbers, outcomes, what changed.
5. **Forward look** — What's next? (optional)

**Example hooks:**
- "The team shipped [feature] this week — and the numbers are already moving."
- "[Person] just hit a milestone worth celebrating."
- "We set out to [goal]. Here's what happened."

### Technical Tip / Insight

For sharing learnings, patterns, tools, or approaches.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** — The problem or question that prompted the insight.
2. **Context** — Why this matters, who runs into this.
3. **The insight** — Clear, actionable explanation.
4. **Example** — Code snippet, screenshot, or concrete illustration.
5. **Takeaway** — One sentence summary of what to do differently.

**Example hooks:**
- "We kept running into [problem]. Here's the pattern that fixed it."
- "One change to our [process/tool] saved us [time/effort] per week."
- "If you're doing [common thing], try this instead."

### Team Update / Announcement

For project updates, org changes, process changes, or calls to action.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** — What's changing and why it matters.
2. **Details** — The specifics: what, when, who's affected.
3. **Rationale** — Why this decision was made.
4. **Action items** — What the reader should do (if anything).
5. **Questions** — Where to ask follow-ups.

**Example hooks:**
- "Starting next week, [thing] is changing. Here's what you need to know."
- "We're trying something new with [process]. Here's the plan."
- "Quick update on [project] — we're at [milestone]."

## Engagement Data & Writing Guidelines

These patterns are drawn from high-performing Vault posts across Shopify. Use them.

### What Makes Posts Perform

| Signal | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Strong opening hook | 2-3x more engagement than buried ledes |
| Specific numbers | Posts with metrics get significantly more reactions |
| Named individuals | Personal recognition drives comments and shares |
| Short paragraphs | 2-3 sentences max per paragraph; walls of text get skipped |
| Clear structure | Headers, bullets, and whitespace improve readability |
| Authentic voice | Posts that sound like a person outperform posts that sound like a memo |

### Hook Patterns That Work

1. **The Result First** — "We reduced [metric] by 40% this quarter."
2. **The Surprising Fact** — "Most teams do [X]. We tried the opposite."
3. **The Question** — "What happens when you [unexpected approach]?"
4. **The Milestone** — "[Person/Team] just shipped their [Nth] feature this year."
5. **The Contrast** — "Before: [old state]. After: [new state]."
6. **The Direct Address** — "If you work on [area], this affects you."

### Formatting Rules

- **Bold** key names, metrics, and takeaways.
- Use bullet lists for 3+ related items.
- Use numbered lists for sequential steps or ranked items.
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Use `---` sparingly to separate major sections in longer posts.
- Link to artifacts (PRs, docs, dashboards) rather than describing them.

### Quantified Impact

When describing impact, be specific:

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Improved performance" | "Reduced p99 latency from 800ms to 120ms" |
| "Helped the team" | "Unblocked 3 teams waiting on this API" |
| "Shipped quickly" | "Went from idea to production in 4 days" |
| "Good quarter" | "Hit 112% of target with 15% fewer resources" |

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **The Humble Brag** — "I know this isn't a big deal, but..." (If it's worth posting, own it.)
- **The Wall of Text** — No structure, no headers, no breathing room.
- **The Vague Win** — "Great work everyone!" with no specifics.
- **The Jargon Dump** — Acronyms and internal terms without context.
- **The Premature Post** — Announcing something before it's confirmed or shipped.
- **The Ghost Credit** — Describing work without naming who did it.
- **The Emoji Overload** — One or two well-placed emoji are fine. A dozen is noise.

## Pre-Publish Checklist

Run through this before presenting the final draft:

- [ ] **Accuracy** — Every fact, metric, and date is verified.
- [ ] **Attribution** — Everyone who contributed is named.
- [ ] **Clarity** — A reader outside the team can understand the post.
- [ ] **Hook** — The first sentence earns the second sentence.
- [ ] **Length** — The post is as short as it can be without losing meaning.
- [ ] **Links** — All referenced artifacts are linked.
- [ ] **Tone** — Sounds like Shaun, not a press release.
- [ ] **Action** — If the reader should do something, it's explicit.

## Safety Notes

- **No fabrication.** If you don't have a number, say so. Don't invent metrics.
- **Iterate, don't ship.** Always present drafts for Shaun's review. Never post directly.
- **Verify names.** Use Vault tools to confirm spelling and profiles before tagging.
- **Check timing.** Don't announce things that haven't shipped or been approved.
- **Respect confidentiality.** If unsure whether something is shareable, ask first.
